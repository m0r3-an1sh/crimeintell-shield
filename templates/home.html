Criminal Actvities

Criminal activity refers to any actions or behaviors that are prohibited by criminal law and punishable by the state.

Murders and Assaults.

Murder is a criminal offense that involves the intentional killing of another person. It is considered to be the most serious of all criminal offenses, punishable by life imprisonment or the death penalty in many countries.

Kidnapping and Abductions.

Kidnapping is a criminal offense that involves the illegal confinement, abduction, or imprisonment of another person against their will. It is considered a serious crime.

Non-consensual sexual crimes

The criminal offenses that involve sexual contact or behavior without the consent of the victim. These crimes include rape, molestation, sexual harassment, and sexual assault. They are serious crimes.

Theft and Dacoity

Theft is a criminal offense that involves the unauthorized taking of property belonging to another person. Depending on the value of the stolen property and the methods used, theft can be punishable by fines, imprisonment, or both.

Surveillance cameras:

Surveillance cameras can be used to monitor public spaces and deter criminal activity. They can also be used to identify suspects and gather evidence after a crime has been committed.

  • The presence of surveillance cameras can deter criminal activity
  • Surveillance cameras can provide valuable evidence for investigations and can be used to identify suspects and track their movements.
  • With the help of internet and smart technology, these cameras can be remotely monitored which can be helpful for security personnel.

GPS tracking

GPS tracking can be used to track the movements of individuals, vehicles, and assets, helping to prevent crimes such as theft and kidnapping.

  • Can be used to track and recover stolen vehicles or assets
  • Allows law enforcement to monitor the movements of suspects
  • Can be used to locate missing persons
  • Can provide evidence for prosecution
  • Can help in investigations by providing location data for crime scenes.

Biometrics

  • Can be used to identify suspects and criminals through fingerprint, facial recognition, DNA analysis, and other methods.
